Apple Pie



Description

An apple pie is a fruit pie in which the principal filling is apples. It is generally has shortcrust pastry both above and below the filling.

Ingredients

  • Apples: This recipe calls for eight small Granny Smith apples.
  • Apples: This recipe calls for eight small Granny Smith apples.
  • Sugars: A blend of white and brown sugar creates the perfect sweet flavor with a hint of warmth.
  • Pie crust: Use a store-bought double crust pie pastry or make your own at home.

Steps

  1. Slice apples evenly: Thin, uniform slices help the pie bake evenly and prevent crunchy spots.
  2. Start hot, then reduce the heat: The high initial temperature helps set the crust, while the lower temperature finishes cooking the apples until tender.
  3. Protect the crust if needed: If the edges brown too quickly, loosely cover them with foil during the final bake time.
  4. Let the pie rest. For best results, allow the pie to cool for at least 2 hours before slicing so the filling can set properly.
